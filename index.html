Our Challenge

Our chosen challenge is to design a community space using end of life shipping containers to benefit our local community.

Our Aims

To reduce homelessness

By building an enclosed community with used shipyard containers.

Provide a second chance to those in need

By encouraging mental and academic growth through support and education sub-programs.

And improve the living experience for everyone in the community

By giving people new found hope for the future and nurturing a sense of community in everyone involved in the program.

Our journey over the course of the design of our solution to this challenge has helped each of us develop and grow in more ways than one. We developed our unique meta skills and personalities while overcoming some of the more challenging aspects of the design of our solution.

Throught the design of our dolution we found, that our local community suffered from increasing homelessness and poverty (with Glasgow being shown as 'the homeless capital of Scotland' by the 'Homelessness in Scotland 24/25 report').

Knowing this we set out to find a solution. Eventually we settled on repurposing old shipping containers as temporary housing for those in financial struggle. Building on this we came up with he idea to not only house the homeless and destitute, but to give them the tools to continue living without having the risk of returning to their previous living conditions. To achieve this we decided to incorporate educational programs and temporary financial support past an individuals stay in our housing, into our solution.

We also decided having security measures to stop disturbances, or the smuggling of illicit substances would be ideal. Additionaly we added addiction rehabiliation to our list of support programs.
